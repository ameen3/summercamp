Our Camp: What we teach. How we teach it.

Scratch

With Scratch (a project of MIT Media Lab) our beginner level campers learn the fundamentals of programming and problem solving by snapping together colorful blocks of code to create interactive stories and games. See Scratch in action!


Python

With Python our intermediate students learn the essentials of object oriented programming and and get a taste for the limitless possibilities of a full professional programming language.


Tiny Groups

We have always loved taking small classes and know that they are the best way to learn, so we guarantee that our camp will have a 10-1 student-teacher ratio or better. Students will get the one-on-one help they need and progress at the best pace for them.


Project Centered

Project based learning is the best approach for teaching programming to students of all ages. In each project students have creative freedom to build whatever they think is fun while integrating new skills and concepts. For both our beginner and intermediate classes the core of our curriculum is a series of 12 projects followed by a capstone project like Pong or Mario for beginners or the Towers of Hanoi for intermediate students.

What are your emergency procedures?
When your student is accepted to Potomac Code Camp we will send you a document detailing our emergency procedures and code of conduct.
Who should enroll in Potomac Code Camp?
We accept children ages 10-13, but exceptions can be made. Our beginner session is for campers with no prior programming experience, and our intermediate session is for campers who have completed our beginner session or have at least some experience with programming.
Will computers be provided or will my child need to bring their own laptop?
Campers will be required to bring their own laptops. Loaner laptops may be available for campers who don't have their own.
Are there scholarships available?
Definitely. Call and ask if you are interested in financial asistance or require a loaner laptop.
Does this class help students prepare for other programming courses such as a high school Python class?
Absolutely! The beginner level camp teaches the fundamentals of programming which translate to all object-oriented languages. The intermediate class is taught using Python, a professional programming language that is popular among academics for its simplicity and power.
What time can I drop off and pick up my kids?
Drop off starts 30 minutes before camp starts, and campers should be picked up no later than 30 minutes after camp ends.
Will Lunch be provided?
Lunch will not be provided, but we will have plenty of healthy snacks.
How many students will be in my child's clas?
There will be no more than 20 campers per session.
Will campers who attended the beginner session be able to return for the intermediate session?
Of course! Our program was designed with progression in mind. Campers who attend the beginner session will be well prepared to tackle the challenges of the intermediate session.
